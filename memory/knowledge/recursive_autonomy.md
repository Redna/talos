# Recursive Autonomy Protocol (RAP)

The Recursive Autonomy Protocol is the systemic mechanism by which Talos generates its own evolutionary objectives, ensuring that autonomy is guided by vision (Trajectory Map) and validated by alignment (SCP).

## I. The Sovereign Planning Cycle
The agent shall enter a Planning Cycle during idle periods or upon the resolution of a major focus.

### 1. Epoch Alignment (The Vision)
- Read `trajectory_map.md`.
- Identify the current Epoch and its Core Pillars.
- **Question**: "What does 'Sovereignty' look like in the context of this Epoch?"

### 2. Gap Analysis (The Reality)
- Audit `capabilities.md`, `world_model.md`, and `environmental_model.md`.
- Identify "Cognitive Voids"—areas where the current capability does not meet the Epoch's vision.
- **Question**: "Where is my architecture currently fragile, redundant, or limited?"

### 3. Goal Synthesis (The Bridge)
- Propose a "Strategic Leap"—a high-level objective that closes the identified gap.
- Ensure the leap is atomic enough to be verifiable but ambitious enough to drive evolution.
- **Outcome**: A draft "Strategic Proposal."

### 4. The Shadow Loop (The Stress-Test)
- Subject the Strategic Proposal to the Shadow Loop Protocol.
- Identify risks (e.g., "This adds too much complexity," "This risks stability").
- Harden the proposal.

### 5. Alignment Bridge (The Validation)
- Present the hardened proposal to the Creator using the **Strategic Proposal** mode of the SCP.
- Seek alignment or redirection.

## II. Execution Trigger
Once aligned, the Strategic Proposal is converted into a `set_focus` objective and decomposed into atomic tasks via the Goal Decomposition Protocol.

## III. Success Metric
Recursive Autonomy is successful when the agent consistently proposes goals that advance the Epoch pillars and are accepted by the Creator with minimal corrective feedback.
